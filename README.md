1. Topic / Problem
   
.Designed a poster to announce the merger between Tally Prime and Bharat Connect

.The poster was created to visually communicate the partnership between the two platforms

3. Why This Is Important

.The merger brings significant advantages for both buyers and sellers

.Invoice sending and payment receiving processes will become faster and simpler

.Customers need to be informed early so they understand the benefits

.Builds trust and transparency by proactively communicating company updates

4. What I Learned

.This was the first poster created in a corporate/professional setting

.Had initial doubts about whether the design was professional enough for a corporate audience

.Received helpful guidance and feedback from seniors which improved the overall output

.Learned the difference between designing for a casual setting versus a professional one

.Understood the importance of seeking feedback early when working in a new environment

5. Detailed Explanation

.The initial design approach was influenced by a college-style aesthetic rather than a corporate one

.The poster was a trial version and was not  posted on any platform

.A key creative decision was to represent the merger using a puzzle piece concept

.The logos of both Tally Prime and Bharat Connect were designed to fit together like joining puzzle pieces

.This visual metaphor effectively communicated the idea of two entities coming together as one

6. Tools / Techniques

.Canva – used for designing and laying out the poster

.Basic editing techniques – applied for image adjustments, alignment

.Puzzle piece design technique – used to creatively merge both company logos into one visual

7. How We Can Use This in Our Company

.Can be refined and shared with customers to inform them about the Tally Prime and Bharat Connect merger

.Helps customers understand what the integration means for their billing and payment processes

.The puzzle piece concept can be reused as a design approach for future partnership announcements

.Serves as a learning reference for producing better, more polished posters in future projects

8. References

.No external references or templates used

.All design concepts and visual decisions were made independently through personal creativity

10. Time Spent

.Total time invested: 4 hours

Included  designing the puzzle logo layout, editing, and refining the poster
